Can I include parts or components from other countries?

Not all of the parts that make up your goods come from {{ context.country_name }}. In order to qualify for preferential treatment, you may be able to include parts that come from other countries.

This depends on the cumulation rules of the trade agreement, which are described below.

Cumulation is the term used to describe a system that allows the origin of materials or processing undertaken in country A to be added to the materials and processing undertaken within country B, so long as they are further processed or added to products originating in country B.

This allows those factors that contributed to the origin of country A materials to be considered as though undertaken in B.

{#

With cumulation although not all preferential agreements enable this, where they do the working or processing carried out in each partner country on originating products does not have to be ‘sufficient working or processing’ as set out in the list rules although it should be beyond minimal processing.

#}
