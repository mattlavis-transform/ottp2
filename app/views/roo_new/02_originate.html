How to work out if your products are classed as 'originating' in {{ context.country_name if context.trade_direction == "import" else "the United Kingdom" }}

{#

Rules of origin are the criteria which determine the national source of a product. They are important as they determine if your trade is eligible for preferential tariff treatment.

#}

This page defines the broad rules that determine if your product originates in {{ context.country_name if context.trade_direction == "import" else "the United Kingdom" }} according to the {{ context.scheme_title }}.

{#

According to the {{ context.scheme_title }}, the following products will be considered as originating in Albania:

#}

The following products are considered as originating in {{ context.country_name if context.trade_direction == "import" else "the United Kingdom" }}:

  • products wholly obtained in {{ context.country_name if context.trade_direction == "import" else "the United Kingdom" }};
  • products obtained in Albania incorporating materials which have not been wholly obtained there, provided that these materials have undergone sufficient working or processing in {{ context.country_name if context.trade_direction == "import" else "the United Kingdom" }} to obtain originating status;
  • items produced using some non-originating materials, provided the product meets the rules specifically associated with commodity {{ context.goods_nomenclature_item_id | format_and_trim_commodity_code | safe }}.

These concepts are defined throughout the next pages, so that you can determine if your product meets the rules of origin.
